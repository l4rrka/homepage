Poem 3

Now we have entered the era of my self-destruction - 12th January 2021. This is the period I generally don’t like to remind myself of. I was apathetic most of the time (shout out to the boy from Poem 1), didn’t see a meaning in my life, and was disgustingly dramatic. Covid played bad jokes with my self-esteem and, lacking any self-confidence, I found myself losing all the friends I acquired during 2018 and 2019. It will be a long time - way until 2023 - when I will finally break out of that doomed loop and find new friends. But it will never be the same again, and now I cannot rely on anybody but myself to provide entertainment and support. Although I might have exaggerated things greatly in my poem, the bare fact is still there - at the end of the day, I taught myself not to be affected by society.

Once again, I’m left alone,
With night light as my only plea.
No voice to echo but my own—
How harsh and cruel this world can be.

I want to scream, to shake the skies,
To tear the silence, loud and clear.
But every word of mine just dies,
Like whispers no one wants to hear.

I dream of warmth, a tender flame,
A love that’s steady, deep, and true.
But all my hopes just feel the same—
Like fleeting stars that fade from view.

All I can do is bow my head,
And softly whisper, "Let me be."
For no one cares to hear me saying
How harsh and cruel this world can be.
