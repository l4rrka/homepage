Poem 1

My life story is launched with the poem I wrote on the 1st March 2019. This was a year of changes; I was gaining, losing, falling in love, and learning from, my new friends. The boy who this poem is written to would be my best friend’s boyfriend who, ironically, was a crush of mine and my other best friend’s too. He was an interesting person - probably the first boy who paid me at least a slight fleck of attention. Once he told me that the fact that boys don’t seem to be interested in me may mean that they’re too scared to make the first move. To this day I don’t know what inspired him to share such a thought - and still I don’t really believe it - but it was something a 13-year-old girl would want to hear. He was quiet and sometimes apathetic - I remember asking him to teach me how to be so cold-hearted. Looking back, we didn’t even text much. I surely wasn’t his type or mature enough to enter relationships - so my feelings were only captured in the poem, where I try to excuse his nature and reflect on my emotions.

I love your quiet, sparing speech.
‘Cause when the words, like rivers, flood,
They pour into despair’s cold reach,
And cut the heart down to the blood.

You’re silent now—I know you are;
You won’t say, “I love you”, it’s true.
But rambling nonsense leaves a scar,
So better save me from this view.

You might believe I waste my breath,
Throwing the words to winds that stray.
I love you quiet, calm as death—
So what else can I humbly say?
