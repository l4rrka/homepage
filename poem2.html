Poem 2

The second poem, produced on the 15th August 2020, is, ironically, another serenade to a boy (don’t worry, I had some girl-related ones too). He was an accident crush I happened to have in a camp - nothing more, but back then it felt as if he meant the world to me. What’s interesting is that he was a web developer - and not a bad one - just at the age of 16. He would spend all the outside time printing lines of code on his laptop and trying to explain how he’s getting paid for that. Such an emotional reaction to falling in love has greatly shaped the way I would behave in the future. It took me years of practice and self-improvement to become the one who people are meant to adore, not the one who instead adores them.

You were simply the one I was meant to adore,
The one I had waited for, wishing for more.
And in the moment when the love filled my eyes,
Your knife struck me deeply – a subtle surprise.

You were like a god, and that is no lie,
My eyes were clouded by love’s sweet sigh.
But all I remember, through time’s cruel dance,
Is you healing my wounds with a bittersweet glance.

No matter how loyal my gaze might have been,
All you could see was “goodbye” within.
The words I might’ve spoken years ago,
With a trembling “don’t miss me,” soft and slow.

You sought the heights, the knowledge, the fame,
While I longed for a quiet, simple flame.
Perhaps it was glory that led us apart,
Or simply the paths we chose from the start.

You were the best, and maybe still are,
For my heart holds your memory, near and far.
How stubbornly, often, with sorrow and care,
You’d wound my soul, unaware, unaware.

It was foolish, naive, a childish mistake,
Yet I can’t help but still feel the ache.
I loved you so deeply, with all my might,
Now all that I wish is to forget you tonight.
